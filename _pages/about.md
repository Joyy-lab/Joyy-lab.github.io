Welcome to my personal website! I am Zhao Su, a PhD student at [School of Astronomy and Space Science](https://astronomy.nju.edu.cn/), [Nanjing Univeristy](https://www.nju.edu.cn/) in China. I am supervised by Prof. Zhiyuan Li. I will graduate at 2026 and hopefully become a postdoctoral researcher at [Department of Astronomy, Tsinghua University](https://astro.tsinghua.edu.cn/en).
Before starting my doctoral research, I achieved my B.S. degree in astronomy from Nanjing University. 

I primarily use numerical simulations, combined with multiwavelength observations to understand the beauty and complexity of the Universe. My research is focused on feeding and feedback of low-luminosity AGNs, which are prevalent in the local Unvierse and represent supermassive black holes at most of their lifetimes. I am also interested in the mysterious intermediate-mass black holes, which are suggested to reside in globular clusters. I am curious about how these black holes accrete and grow within star clusters. Besides, I also investigate stellar feedback processes, such as supernovae and novae eruptions, in the context of the Galactic center.

I come from [Zibo (淄博), Shandong](https://maps.app.goo.gl/knKsotrhyFtDaeNZ8) in eastern China. Zibo has a long history of over 2000 years. Its name is a combination of the Zi (淄) river and the Bo (博) mountain. Zibo is featured by silk and ceramics production as well as special barbecue.
